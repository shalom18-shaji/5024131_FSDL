MOTA BHAI

> The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) is one of India’s two major national political parties and currently the dominant political force at the Centre.
It was founded in 1980, with its ideological roots in Indian nationalism and the philosophy of Hindutva. The BJP emerged as a strong national party in the 1990s, first forming a short-lived government in 1996 under Atal Bihari Vajpayee, and later leading stable coalition governments from 1998 to 2004.
Its biggest political breakthrough came in 2014, when it won a clear majority under Narendra Modi, ending decades of coalition dependence.
The party repeated its majority win in 2019 and continues to lead the government after the 2024 elections through the NDA alliance.
The BJP focuses on strong central leadership, national security, economic reforms, infrastructure development, and cultural identity.
Over the years, it has expanded its presence across most Indian states, transforming from a cadre-based party into a pan-India political organization.
