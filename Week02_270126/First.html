MOTA BHAI

The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) is one of India’s two major national political parties and is currently the most influential political force at the Centre. Founded in 1980, the party draws its ideological inspiration from Indian nationalism and the philosophy of Hindutva, emphasizing cultural identity, national unity, and self-reliance. Over the decades, the BJP has evolved from a relatively small political organization into a dominant national party with a strong organizational structure and grassroots presence across the country.

A major turning point in the party’s history occurred in 2014, when the BJP secured a clear parliamentary majority under the leadership of Narendra Modi. This victory marked a decisive shift from coalition-era politics to strong central leadership. The party repeated its majority win in 2019 and continues to lead the government after the 2024 elections through the NDA alliance, reinforcing its long-term dominance in Indian politics.

In conclusion, the Bharatiya Janata Party has played a transformative role in reshaping the political landscape of India. By moving from fragmented coalition politics to sustained electoral dominance, it has emerged as one of the most powerful and influential parties in the democratic history of the country.
